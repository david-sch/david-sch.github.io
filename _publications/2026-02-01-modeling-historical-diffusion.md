We develop a generative diffusion method to directly estimate how geographic characteristics affect the speed of historical diffusion. Existing approaches infer the diffusion process indirectly, from average speeds or from models tailored to a single geographic feature; our framework instead combines many geographic layers and recovers the distinct contribution of each. We fit the model to archaeobotanical arrival times for the spread of domesticated crops, recovering baseline diffusion speeds and the role of geography in accelerating or retarding the spread of agriculture from centers of domestication.
