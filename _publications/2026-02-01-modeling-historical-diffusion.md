We develop and estimate a structural model of the spatial diffusion of domesticated crops, fitting it to archaeobotanical arrival times for wheat, rice, and maize. The model recovers baseline diffusion speeds and the role of geography in accelerating or retarding the spread of agriculture from centers of domestication.
