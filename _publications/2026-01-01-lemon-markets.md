Road traffic accidents in poorly regulated public transit systems are a leading cause of death in lowand middle-income countries. We collect data on safe driving practices using a newly developed tracking device installed in minibuses operating from one of Kenya’s busiest bus stations. We randomly provide some passengers with information on the safest firm, but they do not respond to this private safety information. In the second phase of the experiment, we publicly advertise our tracking e↵orts with large banners. This leads to a noticeable shift in passenger choice towards the safest firm, prompting some firms to significantly improve their safety. We develop a game-theoretic model illustrating how consumers and firms respond strategically to the safety competition triggered by the large banners.
